1. Rakuten — Best Overall Cashback App

Rakuten (formerly Ebates) is the gold standard of cashback apps and has been since it launched in 1999. The model is simple: you click through Rakuten before shopping at any of its 3,500+ partner stores, and you earn a percentage of your purchase as cashback. Rakuten has paid out over $3.7 billion in cashback to its members since inception.

How It Works

Install the Rakuten browser extension (available for Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge) or use the mobile app. When you visit a participating store's website, the extension automatically pops up to let you know cashback is available. Click "Activate" and shop normally. Rakuten tracks your purchase and credits the cashback to your account. You get paid quarterly via check or PayPal.

Cashback Rates

Rates vary by retailer and fluctuate based on seasonal promotions. Typical ranges in 2026 include:

  • Fashion: 3-15% (Nordstrom, Macy's, Nike, Adidas)
  • Electronics: 1-5% (Best Buy, Samsung, Dell)
  • Travel: 2-8% (Hotels.com, Expedia, Booking.com)
  • Food delivery: 1-6% (DoorDash, Instacart)
  • Home goods: 2-10% (Wayfair, Overstock, Bed Bath & Beyond)

Rakuten frequently runs double cashback events, especially during Black Friday, Cyber Monday, and back-to-school season. During these promotions, rates can jump to 10-20% at major retailers.

Pros

  • 3,500+ partner stores
  • Reliable quarterly payouts
  • Browser extension is seamless
  • In-store cashback via linked card
  • $30 welcome bonus for new members

Cons

  • Quarterly payouts (not instant)
  • Some tracked purchases fail
  • Cashback rates vary frequently
  • No receipt scanning feature

Average Annual Savings

Based on user data, active Rakuten members who do moderate online shopping earn between $150 and $400 per year. Heavy online shoppers who consistently click through Rakuten for fashion, travel, and home purchases report $500 to $1,000+ in annual cashback. The key is making Rakuten your default habit for every online purchase.

2. Ibotta — Best for Grocery Cashback

Ibotta is the dominant cashback app for grocery shopping. Unlike Rakuten which focuses on online retail, Ibotta specializes in item-level cashback on specific grocery products. Before you shop, browse available offers and add them to your list. After shopping, scan your receipt, and Ibotta credits cashback for any qualifying items you purchased.

How It Works

Open the Ibotta app, browse available offers, and tap to add them to your account. Offers are organized by retailer and product category. Shop at any participating store (Walmart, Target, Kroger, Costco, CVS, Walgreens, and hundreds more), then scan your receipt in the app. Ibotta uses OCR to verify your purchases and credits cashback within 24 hours. You can also link your store loyalty cards for automatic cashback without receipt scanning.

Typical Savings

  • Per shopping trip: $2 to $8 depending on available offers
  • Per month: $10 to $30 for regular grocery shoppers
  • Per year: $120 to $360 for consistent users
  • Bonus earnings: Ibotta frequently offers bonuses for completing sets of related purchases (e.g., buy 5 specific products, earn $5 bonus)

Pros

  • Best grocery cashback available
  • Works at nearly all major retailers
  • Loyalty card linking for automatic credit
  • Bonus offers boost earnings
  • Low $20 cashout minimum

Cons

  • Requires pre-activating offers
  • Receipt scanning can be finicky
  • Offers change frequently
  • Some offers are for specific brands only

Ibotta also expanded into online cashback similar to Rakuten, offering percentage-based cashback at hundreds of online retailers. This makes it a dual-purpose app: grocery cashback through receipt scanning and online cashback through its browser extension or app links.

3. Fetch Rewards — Easiest Cashback App to Use

Fetch Rewards takes a fundamentally different approach to cashback: instead of requiring you to activate specific offers or shop at specific stores, you simply scan any receipt from any store, and you earn points. Every receipt earns points. Every time. No pre-selection, no brand requirements, no store restrictions. This simplicity is what makes Fetch the easiest cashback app on the market.

How It Works

Download the Fetch Rewards app, create an account, and start scanning receipts. That is literally it. Every receipt from a grocery store, convenience store, restaurant, gas station, or retail store earns you base points. Receipts containing products from Fetch's partner brands earn bonus points. Points convert to gift cards from hundreds of retailers including Amazon, Target, Starbucks, Walmart, and more.

Points System

  • Base points: Every receipt earns at least 25 points
  • Partner brand bonuses: Products from brands like Coca-Cola, General Mills, Kraft, and Unilever earn 25-250 bonus points per item
  • Special offers: Featured offers can earn 500-5,000 points for specific purchases
  • Redemption: 1,000 points = $1 in gift cards. Minimum redemption is 3,000 points ($3)

Pros

  • Scan any receipt, earn points
  • No offer pre-activation needed
  • Works at any store
  • Low $3 minimum redemption
  • Very easy to use

Cons

  • Lower value per receipt than Ibotta
  • Gift cards only (no cash)
  • Points expire after 90 days inactive
  • Partner brands vary

Fetch Rewards works best as a complementary app alongside Ibotta. Scan the same receipt in both apps: use Ibotta for specific item cashback, then scan again in Fetch for additional points. There is no conflict between the two, and using both together typically doubles your grocery savings.

4. TopCashback — Highest Cashback Rates

Where Rakuten might offer 3% at a particular retailer, TopCashback often offers 5-8% at the same store. The catch is that TopCashback has a smaller selection of retailers and a longer payout process.

Key Features

  • Higher rates: TopCashback guarantees it will match or beat any competitor's cashback rate

  • Tracking period: Cashback typically takes 1-3 months to become "payable" after a purchase, as TopCashback waits for the retailer to confirm the sale
  • Partner stores: Over 4,000 retailers including most major brands

It also earns you Shopping Credits (rewards) on purchases that you can redeem for gift cards. Additionally, it shows price comparisons from other retailers so you can verify you are getting the best deal.

7. Dosh — Best Passive Cashback

How It Works

Download the Dosh app, create an account, and link your credit or debit cards. When your balance reaches $25, you can cash out to PayPal, Venmo, or directly to your bank account.

How Cashback Apps Make Money (And Why They Are Free)

Understanding the business model behind cashback apps helps explain why they are free and why they can afford to give you money back on purchases. The answer is affiliate commissions.

When a retailer like Nike wants to drive sales, they partner with cashback platforms like Rakuten. Nike agrees to pay Rakuten a commission (let us say 10%) for every sale that Rakuten sends to Nike's website. When you click through Rakuten to buy $100 shoes from Nike, Nike pays Rakuten $10 in commission. Rakuten keeps a portion (say $5) and passes the rest ($5) to you as cashback.

This is a win-win-win situation. Nike gets a new customer. Rakuten earns a commission. You get money back on a purchase you were going to make anyway. The key insight is that you should never buy something just because cashback is available. The best way to use cashback apps is to activate them for purchases you were already planning to make.

Why Rates Vary by Retailer

Different retailers pay different commission rates to cashback platforms based on their profit margins and marketing budgets. Fashion retailers with high margins might pay 10-15% commissions, which is why you see high cashback rates at clothing stores. Electronics retailers with thin margins might only pay 1-3%, resulting in lower cashback rates. Travel companies, especially hotels, often pay high commissions because the lifetime value of a customer is significant.

How to Stack Cashback for Maximum Savings

The real power of cashback apps comes from stacking. Because different apps use different tracking mechanisms, you can often earn cashback from multiple sources on a single purchase. Here is how to maximize your savings on every transaction.

The Ultimate Stacking Strategy

  1. Use a cashback credit card: Start with a card that earns 1-5% cashback on purchases (Chase Freedom, Citi Double Cash, etc.)
  2. Click through Rakuten or TopCashback: Before buying online, activate cashback through your preferred platform
  3. Let Capital One Shopping or Honey apply coupons: At checkout, let your browser extension find the best coupon codes
  4. For groceries, pre-activate Ibotta offers: Check Ibotta for available cashback on items you plan to buy
  5. Scan your receipt in Fetch Rewards: After any in-store purchase, scan the receipt for additional points
  6. Have Dosh linked to your card: If the store is a Dosh partner, you earn automatic cashback too

Using this full stack, it is possible to earn 10-20% total savings on a single purchase through a combination of credit card rewards, portal cashback, coupon codes, item-level rebates, and receipt points. Even if you only use two or three of these layers, you are significantly ahead of someone using no cashback tools at all.

Pro Tip: Never Buy Just for Cashback

Cashback should be a bonus on purchases you were already going to make. Buying a $200 item you do not need to earn $10 in cashback is not saving money. It is spending $190. Only activate cashback for items already on your shopping list.

Common Cashback Mistakes to Avoid

1. Forgetting to Activate Before Buying

The most common mistake is completing a purchase before activating your cashback app. If you buy through Nike.com without first clicking through Rakuten, you earn zero cashback. Most cashback platforms cannot retroactively credit purchases. The browser extensions help by reminding you, but build the habit of always checking for cashback before clicking "Buy."

2. Using Conflicting Coupon Codes

Sometimes applying a third-party coupon code at checkout cancels the affiliate tracking that enables your cashback. If you found a coupon from an external source, it might override Rakuten's tracking cookie. When in doubt, use the coupon codes found through your cashback platform (like Rakuten's built-in coupons) rather than external codes.

3. Not Comparing Cashback Rates Across Platforms

Before making a major purchase, check the cashback rate at both Rakuten and TopCashback. The difference can be significant. A $500 purchase with 5% cashback at one platform versus 10% at another is a $25 difference. For big purchases, the two minutes spent comparing rates pays off handsomely.

4. Letting Points Expire

Some cashback apps have expiration policies. Fetch Rewards points expire after 90 days of account inactivity. Honey Gold expires after 12 months. Log in periodically to keep your accounts active and redeem your rewards before they disappear.

5. Ignoring In-Store Cashback

Many people use cashback apps for online shopping but forget about in-store opportunities. Ibotta, Dosh, and Rakuten all offer in-store cashback. Link your cards, scan your receipts, and do not leave money on the table when shopping in person.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money can you actually save with cashback apps?

The average active user saves between $150 and $600 per year using cashback apps, depending on spending habits and how many apps they use. Heavy online shoppers who stack multiple cashback apps with credit card rewards can save over $1,000 annually. Grocery-focused apps like Ibotta and Fetch Rewards typically save $10 to $30 per month for regular users.

Are cashback apps safe to use?

The major cashback apps listed in this guide (Rakuten, Ibotta, Fetch Rewards, TopCashback, Capital One Shopping) are safe and legitimate. They make money through affiliate commissions from retailers, not by selling your personal data. However, you should always review the privacy policy of any app before installing it. Be especially cautious of lesser-known apps that request excessive device permissions or access to contacts and messages.

Can you use multiple cashback apps at once?

Yes, and you should. Different cashback apps work through different mechanisms, so stacking them is often possible. Use Rakuten or TopCashback for online portal cashback, scan your receipt with Ibotta for item-level rebates, then scan the same receipt with Fetch Rewards for points. You can also stack all of these with credit card cashback rewards. The key is understanding which apps can coexist without conflicting.

How do cashback apps make money?

Cashback apps earn affiliate commissions from retailers. When you click through Rakuten to buy something at a store, the retailer pays Rakuten a commission for referring the sale. Rakuten then shares a portion of that commission with you as cashback. The retailer gets a sale, the cashback app earns a commission, and you get money back. This model has been working since the late 1990s and is well-established in e-commerce.

What is the best cashback app for groceries?

Ibotta is the best cashback app for groceries. It offers item-level cashback on specific products at most major grocery chains including Walmart, Kroger, Target, Costco, and hundreds of others. Fetch Rewards is also excellent for groceries because it gives you points for scanning any receipt regardless of what you buy. For maximum grocery savings, use both apps: activate Ibotta offers before shopping, then scan your receipt in both Ibotta and Fetch Rewards after checkout.

How long does it take to get paid from cashback apps?

Payout timelines vary significantly by app. Rakuten pays quarterly via check or PayPal (payments go out in February, May, August, and November). Ibotta lets you cash out once you reach $20, with funds arriving in your PayPal or Venmo within 24 hours. Fetch Rewards lets you redeem points for gift cards instantly once you hit 3,000 points. TopCashback has a tracking period of 1-3 months before cashback becomes payable. Dosh automatically cashes you out when your balance reaches $25.
